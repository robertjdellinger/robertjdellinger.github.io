## Introduction

Conducting scientific research that directly informs environmental policy, remediation decisions, and regulatory frameworks, centering community needs and environmental justice.

## Methods

Applied research approaches including environmental compliance monitoring, natural resource damage assessment (NRDA), and stakeholder-engaged project design.

## Findings

Science conducted in direct response to policy needs and community priorities can effectively bridge the gap between research and action, particularly when designed with use-informed approaches.

## Why it matters

Policy-driven science ensures research addresses pressing environmental challenges and supports communities in their efforts to achieve environmental justice and ecosystem restoration.
