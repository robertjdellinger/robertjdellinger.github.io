## Introduction

Study abroad research program examining wetland ecology and community science approaches in the Lake Atitlan watershed, Guatemala, focusing on the intersection of ecological processes and social dynamics.

## Methods

Field-based ecological surveys combined with community engagement and participatory research methods to understand wetland function and human-environment relationships.

## Findings

Integrated approaches combining ecological monitoring with community knowledge reveal complex interactions between land use change, water quality, and ecosystem services in the Lake Atitlan region.

## Why it matters

Understanding the coupled human-natural systems in Lake Atitlan provides insights into sustainable watershed management and the role of community science in environmental stewardship.
